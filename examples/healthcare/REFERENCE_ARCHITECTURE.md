\# Healthcare Governance Reference Architecture



\## Purpose



This document demonstrates how pramanasystems deterministic governance infrastructure may be used in healthcare AI workflows.



The architecture preserves:



\- deterministic governance enforcement

\- replay-safe execution

\- immutable provenance

\- independently reproducible governance evidence

\- portable verification



AI systems generate signals.



pramanasystems governs deterministic enforcement.

Example Workflow

AI Evaluation Layer



Healthcare AI systems may perform:



symptom extraction

risk classification

recommendation generation

urgency scoring

transcription analysis



These systems remain probabilistic.



AI output is treated as governed signal generation.



Governed Signals



Signals may include:



escalation\_required

severity\_level

emergency\_indicator

identity\_verified

consent\_verified



Signals are deterministic governance inputs.



Policy Governance Layer



Governance policies determine:



escalation requirements

authorization rules

runtime requirements

compatibility requirements

enforcement constraints



Policies remain deterministic.



Deterministic Enforcement Layer



pramanasystems may deterministically enforce:



escalation routing

human-review requirements

authorization decisions

audit recording

replay protection

runtime validation



Deterministic enforcement remains independently reproducible.



Execution Attestations



Each governance execution produces:



signed execution evidence

immutable provenance

governance lineage

runtime identity evidence



Execution evidence remains portable and independently verifiable.



Independent Verification



External systems may independently validate:



execution provenance

attestation signatures

runtime compatibility

governance lineage

release provenance



Verification does not require centralized infrastructure trust.



Governance Principle



pramanasystems does NOT determine medical truth.



The platform governs deterministic enforcement around AI-generated signals.



Operational Principle



Healthcare governance workflows require deterministic trust, reproducibility, and independently verifiable enforcement evidence.
